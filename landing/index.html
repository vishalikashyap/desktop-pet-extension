Desktop Pet Source on GitHub

A tiny pet that lives on every page you browse.

Desktop Pet is a free, open-source browser extension. Pick a cat, dog, panda, rabbit, or fox — it wanders your screen, sleeps, stretches, dances, waves, and leaves little paw prints behind it. Drag it, click it, or just let it live its life while you work.

What it does

🐱🐶🐼🐰🦊

Five pets to choose from

Cat, dog, panda, rabbit, or fox — each hand-built as an animated SVG rig, not a static image.

💃

Eight living behaviors

Walking, sleeping, sitting, looking around, stretching, jumping, waving, and dancing — picked at random so it never feels scripted.

🐾

Footprint trail

Leaves a fading trail of paw prints behind it as it walks — wherever it roams on the page.

❤️

Reacts to you

Hover for a smile, click for a heart, double-click for a dance, or drag it anywhere on the page.

💬

Speech bubbles

Drops the occasional friendly reminder — "drink water", "keep coding" — at a frequency you control.

🔊

Its own little voice

Each species makes a short synthesized sound, generated on the fly — no audio files required.

📍

Lives where you want

Pin it to a corner, or let it free-roam and drag it anywhere — its spot is remembered.

🎛️

Fully customizable

Size, animation speed, opacity, sound, theme, walk direction, and speech frequency, all from one settings panel.

Install it (takes under a minute)

  1. Download the zip above and unzip it anywhere.
  2. Open chrome://extensions (or edge://extensions).
  3. Turn on Developer mode (top-right toggle).
  4. Click Load unpacked and select the unzipped folder.
  5. Open any website — your pet is already there.

Not yet on the Chrome Web Store, so this manual step is required — it's the same "Developer mode" flow used by any unpublished extension, and Chrome will remember it until you remove the extension yourself.

On your phone

There's no equivalent of a browser extension on mobile, so the phone version is a standalone installable web app instead. Open the mobile app on your phone and use your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option — it'll behave like a real app icon, working offline once installed.
